# My recommendation

You've already made one good strategic decision with GS Error Reporter: you shipped a solid foundation, then improved it over time instead of waiting for perfection.

I think GS Tech Info is at the same point now.

You've built the difficult parts—the architecture, authentication, publishing workflow, moderation, and Git-backed content system. Those are the foundations that are expensive to change later.

At this stage, your highest-value work is no longer adding infrastructure. It's publishing excellent content, gathering feedback, and iterating based on real usage.

So yes—I would deploy it quietly, monitor it, write content consistently, and let each future release add one meaningful capability at a time. That's a much stronger path than delaying the launch in pursuit of a "complete" version that may never feel complete.
